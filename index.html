About

I am Lucy. I am trained as a Sustainable Architect. I specialise in:

  • Innovative Design
  • Design Management
  • Sustainable Strategies
  • Project Management

I offer community engagement and advice on facilitating ambitious sustainability targets into achievable outcomes.

Design Services

I can offer architectural services from feasibility through to planning and construction. I have experience across all RIBA workstages. I enjoy creating sustainable site strategies and prioritising human and planetary wellbeing.

I have specialist knowledge in natural materials, low carbon designs and passive principles. I have worked extensively in social housing and have been involved in developing a timber and natural material based MMC (modern methods of construction) panelised system.

Design Management

I can bridge the gap between creative design and delivering within budget. I have experience in co-ordinating multi-disciplinary design teams; and working with build teams to complete pioneering projects with accelerated programmes.

I enjoy aligning high sustainability targets with commercial constraints without compromising quality.

Sustainability Services

Recent projects have had ambitious sustainability targets that I am able to advise on following training from Centre for Alternative Technology (CAT) in Sustainable Architecture.

I do thermal / energy / lighting calculations at early feasibility stages to help inform sustainable design.

Project Management

Co-ordinating design teams and facilitating design meetings and engagement. I can also offer community engagement services. Co-ordination to de-risk projects from an early stage.

I have worked on lots of difficult sites – with underground services or steep slopes – and am used to coming up with innovative solutions to deal with these problems where others have given up.

Sustainability comes as standard and I am used to engaging others to weave their designs into more sustainable and cost effective strategies that enhance developments.

7 Homes for the Homeless

A pioneering social housing project delivering seven homes to expand Places for People's existing hostel. We navigated a challenging 40-degree slope and complex stakeholder requirements. We successfully utilized natural materials within the panelised construction. The project translates ambitious sustainability (social and environmental) into actionable reality, proving that natural materials can be deployed effectively within tight budgets and that those moving in could live with dignity. I was involved in concept to completion of this project. It won the Bristol Civic Design Award on its completion in 2025 which was a privilege to receive. Heating and hot water come from a shared ASHP. The homes are expected to cost just £5 a week in energy and store an estimated 122 tonnes of carbon dioxide in the building envelope.

Housing for refugees

A housing project in Bristol delivering low-carbon, community-focused accommodation for a refugee family as part of the Baptist Church's mission to help meet demand for safe accommodation.

The development has been designed not only to have a very small carbon footprint, but to reduce the impacts of climate change. The materials used in the construction (predominantly timber, straw or other biobased insulation) capture carbon in the fabric of the buildings and can be recycled at the end of their useful life meaning there are very few upfront carbon emissions, and the development is a net store of carbon. Jackpads, rather than traditional concrete foundations, will be used to support the structure – helping to reduce the climate and natural resource impacts of using concrete.

The proposal is to use this space to custom build an annex providing accommodation for a person linked with the church who supports their outreach work.
